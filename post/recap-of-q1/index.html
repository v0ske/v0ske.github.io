Recap of Q1

The year so far

March is coming to an end, and I want to share some reflections on the first few months of 2025. The year started off sunny, in Scotland! Who would’ve thought? Visiting Edinburgh and Dunfermline was an absolute blast. Reconnecting with friends and soaking up the positive energy of nature felt like the perfect reset. We even kicked off the year with a New Year’s Dive into the icy waters of the Firth of Forth.

In February, Belgium finally got a new government. In my personal opinion: a bit late, but better late than never. Thanks to the great work of our communications team at YATA Belgium, we shared an infographic highlighting the key defense updates. One important nuance: while the official program was just released, geopolitics moved faster. Belgium’s defense budget will increase to 2% of GDP by June this year (Hague Summit) up from around 1.2% (or 1.3% if you’re being generous). And even less if you subtract the cost of military pensions. As our new Minister of Defense, Theo Francken, rightly said: “Si vis pacem, para bellum.”

February also brought the Munich Security Conference, where YATA Belgium was present at various side events. We connected with fellow YATA chapters, military officials, and international relations experts, building bridges, exchanging insights, and strengthening transatlantic bonds.

And beyond Munich: YATA Belgium was honored to be invited by the National Liberal Club to participate in a roundtable on defense policy. A fantastic opportunity to contribute to a forward-looking exchange of ideas on transatlantic security.
